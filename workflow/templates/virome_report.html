Statistical Outlier Detection Methodology
IQR (Interquartile Range) Method

This report uses the IQR statistical method to identify outliers across quality metrics:

  • Q1 (25th percentile): The value below which 25% of samples fall
  • Q3 (75th percentile): The value below which 75% of samples fall
  • IQR = Q3 - Q1: The spread of the middle 50% of samples
  • Outlier thresholds: Values < Q1-1.5×IQR or > Q3+1.5×IQR
Metrics Evaluated

Samples are flagged as outliers based on unusual values in any of these metrics:

  • Quality Score: Overall sample quality assessment
  • Read Retention: Percentage of reads surviving quality control
  • Host Contamination: Percentage of host reads (VLP prep efficiency)
  • rRNA Contamination: Percentage of rRNA reads (biological contamination)
  • Contamination Levels: PhiX, vector, host, and rRNA contamination
Interpreting Outliers
Potential Issues:
  • Library preparation problems
  • Sequencing quality issues
  • Sample contamination (host, rRNA, PhiX, vector)
  • VLP preparation efficiency issues
Biological Variation:
  • Genuine low viral load samples
  • Different sample types
  • Natural biological diversity
  • Expected experimental variation
Recommendation: Review outlier samples individually to determine if they require reprocessing, additional analysis, or represent genuine biological differences.
